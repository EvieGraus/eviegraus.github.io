--- layout: archive title: "Research" permalink: /research/ author_profile: true --- ## Working Papers **Roads and Child Health in Sub-Saharan Africa** *Revise & Resubmit, Journal of Development Economics* with Luisito Bertinelli, Jean-François Maystadt, and Silvia Peracchi [Paper](/files/roads_child_health_SSA_Oct2025.pdf){: .btn .btn--primary .btn--small}
Abstract This paper examines the causal impact of road access on child health in Sub-Saharan Africa between 1980 and 2012 by combining geolocated data on child anthropometric outcomes with spatial data on road networks. To address endogeneity, we employ an instrumental variable approach based on the inconsequential units framework, constructing hypothetical road networks that connect historical cities and active mines. Our results show that closer proximity to paved roads significantly improves child health. The main mechanisms operate through improved healthcare access and utilization, higher household wealth, early signs of structural transformation, and cropland expansion. We find no evidence that these gains are offset by adverse environmental or epidemiological effects of improved road access.
--- ## Work in Progress **Violence Next Door: How Conflict Reconfigures Regional Trade and Development** with Silvia Peracchi
Abstract This project examines how conflict-induced trade shocks affect local development beyond national borders. We exploit the 2002 civil war in Côte d’Ivoire, which temporarily disrupted access to the Port of Abidjan and redirected trade from Mali and Burkina Faso toward alternative West African coastal routes. Preliminary findings suggest persistent local development gains in areas with greater access to affected trade corridors, reflected in increases in settlement area, population density, and nighttime lights, alongside declining cropland.
