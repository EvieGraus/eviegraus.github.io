**Hello!**

I am a PhD candidate in Economics at the University of Luxembourg, working at the intersection of development economics and economic geography.

My research studies how road infrastructure shapes sustainable and inclusive development in Sub-Saharan Africa, one of the world’s least connected regions. I am especially interested in the mechanisms through which roads foster development, who benefits from improved connectivity, and how trade-offs emerge when infrastructure, urbanization, and environmental pressures interact.

Methodologically, I combine geospatial data, large-scale household surveys, and causal econometric methods. My current projects examine how road infrastructure affects child health, regional development, environmental outcomes, and women’s empowerment.
