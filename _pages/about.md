## **Hello!**

I am a PhD candidate in Economics at the University of Luxembourg. My research interests are in Development Economics, Economic Geography, and Environmental Economics.

As part of my dissertation, I study how road infrastructure shapes sustainable and inclusive development in Sub-Saharan Africa. More specifically, my current projects examine how road infrastructure affects child health, regional development, environmental outcomes, and women’s empowerment. I am particularly interested in understanding the trade-offs that emerge when infrastructure, urbanization, and environmental pressures interact.

Methodologically, I combine geospatial data, large-scale household surveys, and causal econometric methods.
